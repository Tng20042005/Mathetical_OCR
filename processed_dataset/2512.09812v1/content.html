Jacob’s ladders, our old formula (1985) and new \zeta-equivalent of the Fermat-Wiles theorem on two-parametric set of lemniscates of Bernoulli

Jan Moser Department of Mathematical Analysis and Numerical Mathematics, Comenius University, Mlynska Dolina M105, 842 48 Bratislava, SLOVAKIA jan.mozer@fmph.uniba.sk
Abstract.

In our paper from 1985 we have constructed two integrals of the Riemann’s function Z^{2}(t) over two disconnected sets with asymptotically equal measures such that these two integrals differ by considerably big excess. In the present paper we use the formula for that excess to construct a new \zeta-equivalent of the Fermat-Wiles theorem on a two-parametric set of lemniscates of Bernoulli.

Riemann zeta-function

2. Jacob’s ladders: notions and basic geometrical properties

2.1.

In this paper we use the following notions of our works [5] – [9]:

  • (a)

    Jacob’s ladder \varphi_{1}(T),

  • (b)

    direct iterations of Jacob’s ladders

    \begin{split}&\varphi_{1}^{0}(t)=t,\ \varphi_{1}^{1}(t)=\varphi_{1}(t),\ % \varphi_{1}^{2}(t)=\varphi_{1}(\varphi_{1}(t)),\dots,\\ &\varphi_{1}^{k}(t)=\varphi_{1}(\varphi_{1}^{k-1}(t))\end{split}

    for every fixed natural number k,

  • (c)

    reverse iterations of Jacob’s ladders

    (2.1) \begin{split}&\varphi_{1}^{-1}(T)=\overset{1}{T},\ \varphi_{1}^{-2}(T)=\varphi% _{1}^{-1}(\overset{1}{T})=\overset{2}{T},\dots,\\ &\varphi_{1}^{-r}(T)=\varphi_{1}^{-1}(\overset{r-1}{T})=\overset{r}{T},\ r=1,% \dots,k,\end{split}

    where, for example,

    (2.2) \varphi_{1}(\overset{r}{T})=\overset{r-1}{T}

    for every fixed k\in\mathbb{N} and every sufficiently big T>0. We also use the properties of the reverse iterations listed below.

    (2.3) \overset{r}{T}-\overset{r-1}{T}\sim(1-c)\pi(\overset{r}{T});\ \pi(\overset{r}{% T})\sim\frac{\overset{r}{T}}{\ln\overset{r}{T}},\ r=1,\dots,k,\ T\to\infty,
    (2.4) \overset{0}{T}=T<\overset{1}{T}(T)<\overset{2}{T}(T)<\dots<\overset{k}{T}(T),

    and

    (2.5) T\sim\overset{1}{T}\sim\overset{2}{T}\sim\dots\sim\overset{k}{T},\ T\to\infty.
Remark 4.

The asymptotic behaviour of the points

\{T,\overset{1}{T},\dots,\overset{k}{T}\}

is as follows: at T\to\infty these points recede unboundedly each from other and all together are receding to infinity. Hence, the set of these points behaves at T\to\infty as one-dimensional Friedmann-Hubble expanding Universe.

2.2.

Let us remind that we have proved666See [9], (3.4). the existence of almost linear increments

(2.6) \begin{split}&\int_{\overset{r-1}{T}}^{\overset{r}{T}}\left|\zeta\left(\frac{1% }{2}+it\right)\right|^{2}{\rm d}t\sim(1-c)\overset{r-1}{T},\\ &r=1,\dots,k,\ T\to\infty,\ \overset{r}{T}=\overset{r}{T}(T)=\varphi_{1}^{-r}(% T)\end{split}

for the Hardy-Littlewood integral (1918)

(2.7) J(T)=\int_{0}^{T}\left|\zeta\left(\frac{1}{2}+it\right)\right|^{2}{\rm d}t.

For completeness, we give here some basic geometrical properties related to Jacob’s ladders. These are generated by the sequence

(2.8) T\to\left\{\overset{r}{T}(T)\right\}_{r=1}^{k}

of reverse iterations of the Jacob’s ladders for every sufficiently big T>0 and every fixed k\in\mathbb{N}.

Property 1.

The sequence (2.8) defines a partition of the segment [T,\overset{k}{T}] as follows

(2.9) |[T,\overset{k}{T}]|=\sum_{r=1}^{k}|[\overset{r-1}{T},\overset{r}{T}]|

on the asymptotically equidistant parts

(2.10) \begin{split}&\overset{r}{T}-\overset{r-1}{T}\sim\overset{r+1}{T}-\overset{r}{% T},\\ &r=1,\dots,k-1,\ T\to\infty.\end{split}
Property 2.

Simultaneously with the Property 1, the sequence (2.8) defines the partition of the integral

(2.11) \int_{T}^{\overset{k}{T}}\left|\zeta\left(\frac{1}{2}+it\right)\right|^{2}{\rm d}t

into the parts

(2.12) \int_{T}^{\overset{k}{T}}\left|\zeta\left(\frac{1}{2}+it\right)\right|^{2}{\rm d% }t=\sum_{r=1}^{k}\int_{\overset{r-1}{T}}^{\overset{r}{T}}\left|\zeta\left(% \frac{1}{2}+it\right)\right|^{2}{\rm d}t,

that are asymptotically equal

(2.13) \int_{\overset{r-1}{T}}^{\overset{r}{T}}\left|\zeta\left(\frac{1}{2}+it\right)% \right|^{2}{\rm d}t\sim\int_{\overset{r}{T}}^{\overset{r+1}{T}}\left|\zeta% \left(\frac{1}{2}+it\right)\right|^{2}{\rm d}t,\ T\to\infty.

It is clear, that (2.10) follows from (2.3) and (2.5) since

(2.14) \overset{r}{T}-\overset{r-1}{T}\sim(1-c)\frac{\overset{r}{T}}{\ln\overset{r}{T% }}\sim(1-c)\frac{T}{\ln T},\ r=1,\dots,k,

while our eq. (2.13) follows from (2.6) and (2.5).

7. Concluding remarks

7.1.

Let us remind that the oval of Cassini is the locus traced out by a point M moving so that the product of its distances from two given points F_{1} and F_{2} is constant

(7.1) |F_{1}M||F_{2}M|=b^{2},

where

(7.2) F_{1}=F_{1}(a,0),\ F_{2}=F_{2}(-a,0).
  • (\alpha)

    In the critical case a=b the curve is known as lemniscate of Bernoulli.

  • (\beta)

    In the case b<a the curve consists of two separated ovals. Each of points F_{1}, F_{2} is surrounded by one of these ovals.

  • (\gamma)

    In the case b>a the curve is an oval with both pints F_{1},F_{2} in its interior.

7.2.

Since we wish to keep a^{2} with

(7.3) a=\left(\frac{4}{\pi}\sin v\right)^{6/5}\left(\frac{1}{8l_{3}}\right)^{1/2},\ % l_{3}>0,\ 0<v\leq\frac{\pi}{2}

on the right-hand side of (7.1), we put

(7.4) |F_{5}M||F_{6}M|=a^{2},

where

(7.5) F_{5}=F_{5}(c,0),\ F_{6}=F_{6}(-c,0).

We, however, do not with to introduce a new parameter c, so we shall consider only following two possibilities

(7.6) c=\frac{1}{2}a,\ c=2a

in (7.4), (7.5).

  • (b)

    for
